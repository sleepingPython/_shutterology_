Wood vs vinyl shutters: which is right for your home?

Choosing between wood and vinyl plantation shutters is one of the most common decisions homeowners face. Both offer excellent light control and privacy — but they suit different rooms and priorities.

Wood shutters

Hardwood shutters deliver a rich, natural look that works beautifully in living rooms, bedrooms, and formal spaces. They can be painted or stained to match your décor and feel substantial when you open and close them.

Wood is best kept out of consistently damp environments unless properly finished. For most living areas, it's the premium choice for aesthetics.

Vinyl shutters

Vinyl shutters are engineered for durability and moisture resistance, making them ideal for kitchens, bathrooms, and laundry rooms. They're easy to wipe clean and tend to be more budget-friendly.

Modern vinyl has come a long way — many styles closely mimic painted wood while standing up to daily wear.

Can you mix both?

Absolutely. Many customers choose wood for main living spaces and vinyl for wet areas. We can match colours across materials for a cohesive look throughout your home.
